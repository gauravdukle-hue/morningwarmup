📰 Deep Dive — Major Market Stories
1
☮️ Iran Ceasefire Extended — Geopolitical Risk Recedes (For Now)
President Trump extended the US-Iran ceasefire beyond Wednesday's original expiration, sending futures sharply higher overnight. Tuesday's session fell 0.63% on the S&P 500 as investors grew nervous that a peace deal would not be struck — reports surfaced that VP JD Vance's trip to join negotiations was paused due to a lack of commitment from Tehran. Shortly after the close, Trump announced the extension, immediately reversing futures. The ceasefire remains fragile. Iran must submit a formal proposal, and a breakdown could trigger a sharp oil price spike and risk-off move. Defense stocks may see modest pressure on de-escalation. The Strait of Hormuz risk premium in oil is easing — watch WTI direction at the open as the key barometer.
⚡ Market Impact: HIGH — Watch oil prices + VIX above 20 as warning signs of renewed risk-off
2
🚗 Tesla Q1 2026 Earnings Tonight — The Mag 7 Starting Gun
Tesla is the first Magnificent Seven company to report Q1 results tonight after the close, making it the most closely watched print of the early earnings season. Q1 deliveries of 358,023 vehicles fell 14% quarter-over-quarter but rose 6% year-over-year. Analysts expect Q1 revenue of $22.3B (+15.5% YoY) and EPS of $0.36 (+33.3% YoY). Investors will focus heavily on: (1) gross margin recovery, (2) robotaxi and autonomous driving timeline updates, (3) energy storage business trajectory, and (4) whether management provides or withdraws FY2026 guidance. UBS flags concerns around EV demand headwinds, energy storage softness, and slower-than-expected Optimus progress. Tesla results will set the tone for growth stocks heading into the super-week of April 27–May 1 when Microsoft, Meta, Amazon, and Alphabet all report.
⚡ Market Impact: VERY HIGH — Tesla earnings are a proxy for EV, AI, and Mag 7 sentiment; miss on margins could pressure Nasdaq
3
🏢 Mega-Cap AI Earnings Next Week — The Super Bowl of 2026
The most consequential earnings stretch of 2026 arrives next week. Microsoft and Meta both report April 29, joined by Amazon and Alphabet. The four hyperscalers combined are expected to spend over $470 billion on capex in 2026, up from $350B in 2025. Microsoft: expected $3.88 EPS on $80.2B revenue (+20.1%/+15.2% YoY); Azure growth and AI Copilot adoption are key. Meta: $8.15 EPS on $58.4B revenue; expects $115–135B capex for 2026 as Zuckerberg pursues "personal superintelligence." Amazon: Q1 results due April 29 — AWS grew 24% YoY last quarter; $200B capex planned in 2026. The Nasdaq closed at record territory heading into this stretch. The central investor question: when does the AI infrastructure build-out start generating measurable ROI?
⚡ Market Impact: VERY HIGH — Next week's prints will define Q2 market direction; any capex disappointment could trigger rotation
4
💊 UnitedHealth Q1 Beat — Managed Care Sector Relief
UnitedHealth surged over 9% after reporting Q1 adjusted EPS of $7.23 vs $6.57 expected — a 10% beat. Revenue of $111.72B also topped the $109.57B estimate. The critical metric was the Medical Benefit Ratio (MCR): it improved in the quarter, signaling the company is regaining control of surging medical costs that have plagued the managed care sector. Management also raised its full-year 2026 earnings outlook. This is a significant relief trade for health insurers, which had been under pressure from cost inflation. Watch CVS/Aetna, Cigna, and Humana for sympathy moves. Health Care sector may see a broad rotation inward.
⚡ Market Impact: HIGH — Signals medical cost normalization; positive for entire managed care industry
5
📊 Q1 Earnings Season: Record Beat Rate, Muted Stock Reactions
With 10% of S&P 500 companies having reported, 88% are beating EPS estimates — well above the 5-year average of 78% and the 10-year average of 76%. Companies are reporting earnings 10.8% above estimates on average. The blended Q1 earnings growth rate stands at 13.2%, marking the 6th consecutive quarter of double-digit YoY growth. Information Technology sector is projected to post +45% EPS growth, Materials +24.2%, Financials +15.1%. Full-year 2026 EPS growth projected at 18%; Barclays recently raised its S&P 500 EPS target to $321. However, despite strong beats, post-earnings price moves have averaged -0.2% (vs +1.0% historically) — a sign that expectations are already priced in. Only "whisper beats" will be rewarded; "in-line beats" may disappoint.
⚡ Market Impact: MODERATE — High beat rate broadly positive, but bar is high; selective stock picking matters more than sector bets

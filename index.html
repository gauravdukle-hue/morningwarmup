#1 Macro Risk Iran War & Strait of Hormuz
The U.S. military blockaded the Strait of Hormuz, sending WTI crude above $100/bbl in March — the highest since 2022. The shock revived inflation fears and forced the Fed to abandon two expected 2026 rate cuts. However, diplomatic progress is accelerating: Trump confirmed calls with Israeli PM Netanyahu and Lebanese President Aoun, and a second round of U.S.–Iran negotiations in Islamabad is being considered. Oil has since fallen sharply toward $89/bbl.

Markets are pricing in a resolution, but any breakdown in talks would re-spike energy prices, hit the consumer, and eliminate the equity rally that has powered the S&P 500 to all-time highs. This remains the single largest binary risk in global markets heading into next week.
#2 Theme Magnificent 7 April Surge & AI ROI
After a brutal Q1 selloff (tech -9.3%, S&P -4.6%), April has seen a historic reversal. The Roundhill Magnificent 7 ETF (MAGS) is on pace for its largest monthly gain on record (+14%), led by Tesla (+15% this week) and Microsoft (+14% — largest weekly gain since 2007).

Q1 2026 earnings are the critical test: investors need to see $100B+ in AI capex begin translating into revenue. Microsoft's Azure cloud and Meta's advertising AI are the early bright spots. Alphabet and Amazon report next week, with AI return on investment the central question. Analysts expect Q2–Q4 2026 EPS growth of 20%+, a high bar that requires continued AI monetization.
#3 Policy Fed Frozen — Iran Effect
The Federal Reserve cut rates to 3.50–3.75% in December 2025 and has remained frozen since. The Iran oil shock refueled inflation fears, eliminating the two cuts markets had priced in for 2026. CME FedWatch now shows rates ending the year unchanged.

SF Fed President Daly says policy is "in a very good place, slightly restrictive" and will remain in wait-and-see mode until the geopolitical situation resolves. Critically, any Iran ceasefire that collapses oil prices could quickly reprice two rate cuts back into the curve — a potential major catalyst for equities and bond markets alike.

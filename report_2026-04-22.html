🔬 Deep Dive — Major Market-Moving Stories
1
Strait of Hormuz Reopens — Largest Geopolitical Relief in Years
After the US military blockaded the Strait of Hormuz pushing WTI briefly above $100/bbl, Iran announced Friday it was reopening the critical waterway after preliminary US-Iran negotiations in Islamabad. Oil suffered its worst weekly decline since April 2020, falling 16%. The ripple effects were dramatic — small caps hit all-time highs, cruise and travel names surged 9%+, and inflation fears eased sharply. A second round of US-Iran talks is being considered imminently. This is the biggest macro risk-off reversal of the year.
⚡ Impact: OIL · TRAVEL · DEFENSE · INFLATION EXPECTATIONS · RATE OUTLOOK
2
Big Bank Q1 Earnings: Trading Wins, Lending Margin Under Pressure
Q1 2026 bank results revealed a consistent theme: markets businesses (trading, dealmaking, investment banking) carried results, while net interest margin (NIM) remains under pressure. JPMorgan reported a 13% profit rise but sold off as spread income disappointed. Citigroup beat on bond trading, equities, and overall revenue — the clearest winner. Bank of America and Wells Fargo lagged, with Wells taking the biggest hit after missing on NII. The divergence between fee-based and spread-based income models is the key read-through for the sector.
⚡ Impact: JPM · C · WFC · BAC · GS · MS · XLF FINANCIALS
3
AI Mega-Capex Cycle: $470B+ in 2026 — This Week Is the Proof Point
The four hyperscalers (Microsoft, Meta, Alphabet, Amazon) are projected to spend over $470 billion on AI infrastructure in 2026, up from ~$350B in 2025. Amazon alone guided $125B+ in capex. This week delivers the defining earnings reports of Q1 season: Tesla Monday, Meta and Microsoft Wednesday, Alphabet Thursday. The market's central question: are these massive AI investments beginning to generate measurable revenue returns, or is the payoff still years away? BNP Paribas economists project US GDP could be 10%+ higher by 2034 from AI-driven productivity.
⚡ Impact: MSFT · META · GOOGL · AMZN · TSLA · NVDA · SMH SEMIS
4
Q1 2026 Earnings Season — Strongest Beat Rate in Years
With 10% of S&P 500 having reported, 88% have beaten EPS estimates — well above the 5-year average of 78% and 10-year average of 76%. The aggregate surprise is 10.8% above estimates vs the 7.3% historical average. FactSet projects Q1 marks the 6th consecutive quarter of double-digit EPS growth for the index. All 11 sectors are projected to show year-over-year revenue growth, led by Information Technology, Communication Services, and Financials. This is tracking as one of the strongest earnings seasons in recent history.
⚡ Impact: BROAD MARKET VALUATIONS · P/E MULTIPLES · FORWARD GUIDANCE
